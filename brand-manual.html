Crude (Display only)
Crude is used exclusively for the Forma wordmark. It is not a text font and must never be used for body copy, headings, or UI elements. Always render as SVG outlines.
Hierarchy Principle
Size and weight do the heavy lifting. Bold for primary, semibold for secondary, light for body. Color variations (dim, muted, ghost) add depth without requiring additional weights.
Tracking
Large display text uses negative tracking (-0.02em to -0.03em) for visual density. Labels and small caps use positive tracking (0.06em to 0.12em) for readability.
Mixing Fonts
Space Grotesk and Space Mono share the same x-height and overall rhythm, making them natural companions. Crude stands apart and should never be combined inline with either.

08 — Graphic Patterns
Texture Library
Square Wave
Stacked horizontal square waves with decreasing opacity. Represents digital audio signals. Use for backgrounds, section dividers, and presentation decks.
Dot Grid
Regular dot grid pattern using gold accent at low opacity. Suggests precision, measurement, and studio craft. Use for subtle background texture.
Crude Blocks
Irregular rectangular blocks at varying opacities. Echoes the Crude typeface geometry. Use for print collateral backgrounds, business cards, and packaging.
Pattern Usage Rules
Patterns serve as secondary visual texture. They should never compete with the logo, wordmark, or primary content. Keep opacity below 25% when used as background fills. Patterns can be used at higher contrast in isolated decorative moments (edges, corners, section breaks).
Color Application
In Palette A materials, patterns use gold (#8b7355) or white (#f0eee8) at reduced opacity. In Palette B materials, substitute steel (#A1AAC8). On light backgrounds, patterns use the dark text color (#1a1714) at reduced opacity.
